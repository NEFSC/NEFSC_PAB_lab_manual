    Atlantic Spotted dolphin

    (Stenella frontalis)

    General Info:

    • There is well documented geographic variation in vocalizations, but vocalizations recorded near the Azores are generally similar to those in the offshore Western Atlantic (Gannier et al, 2020)
    • Whistles are generally lower in fq and shorter in duration than common dolphin (Gannier et al, 2020)
    • Whistles often have a high number of inflection points (Azevedo et al, 2010)
    • Can be hard to tell apart from bottlenose dolphins

    Clicks:

    • ICI:
      • Weak modal ICI, possibly due to overlapping click trains (Frasier et al, 2017)
      • 0.075 sec (Frasier et al, 2017)
    • Whistles:
      • Harmonics often present (Gannier et al, 2020)
      • Groups over the continental shelf are significantly different than those from offshore waters, with lower fq, shorter durations, fewer inflection points, and a greater number of steps (Baron et al, 2008)
    • Frequency:
      • Avg minimal fq 7.3 kHz (Gannier et al, 2020)
      • Avg maximal fq > 16kHz (Gannier et al, 2020)
      • Range 7-16 kHz (Azevedo et al, 2010)
    • Duration:
      • Avg 0.65 (Gannier et al, 2020)
      • Range 0.08-2.07 (Azevedo et al, 2010)
    • Waveform:
      • Generally up swept, although up and down sweep modes are possible (Gannier et al, 2020)
